Who needs a visa and who does not

Visa requirements depend on nationality. Some travelers can enter visa-free for short stays, while others must apply in advance. Always confirm with the Brazilian consulate for your country before booking travel.

What people get wrong: assuming previous entry guarantees future entry, or arriving without proof of onward travel.

Entry procedures and border checks

On arrival, you may be asked for your purpose of visit, accommodation details, and proof of funds. Border officials can deny entry even with a valid visa.

Length of stay and conditions

Short-stay permissions are typically limited to 90 days within a 180-day period, unless your visa specifies otherwise. Extensions are not automatic and must be requested before expiration.

Arrival logistics in Rio Grande do Sul

Most arrivals use airports in Porto Alegre or nearby hubs. Expect customs checks and luggage screening. Keep copies of travel insurance and accommodation confirmations.

What you can and cannot do legally

Tourist or visitor status does not allow employment, paid services, or long-term study. Violations can lead to fines, removal, or entry bans.

Can I change status after entering as a tourist?

In most cases, changes must be initiated from abroad or require strict eligibility. Do not assume you can switch after arrival.

What if my flight is delayed past my stay limit?

You must exit before the authorized stay ends. Overstays can affect future eligibility.
