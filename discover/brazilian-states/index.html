Brazil by State

Brazil is divided into 26 states and one federal district, grouped into five major regions. Each state offers unique climates, economies, cultures, and opportunities for immigrants.

North Region (Norte)

Acre (AC)

Capital: Rio Branco | Population: ~888,000 | Climate: Tropical rainforest

Description:

Acre is located in the Amazon region, known for its vast rainforests, biodiversity, and rubber extraction history. The economy is based on sustainable forestry, agriculture, and eco-tourism. It's a frontier state with indigenous cultures and conservation areas.

Official Link:

www.ac.gov.br

Famous Picture:

Amapá (AP)

Capital: Macapá | Population: ~810,000 | Climate: Equatorial

Description:

Amapá straddles the equator, featuring Amazonian forests, rivers, and French Guiana border. Key industries include mining, fishing, and açaí production. It's home to unique landmarks like the equator monument and national parks.

Official Link:

www.ap.gov.br

Famous Picture:

Amazonas (AM)

Capital: Manaus | Population: ~4,361,000 | Climate: Tropical

Description:

The largest state by area, Amazonas is dominated by the Amazon Rainforest. Manaus is an industrial hub with a free trade zone. Eco-tourism, biodiversity, and river expeditions are major draws.

Official Link:

www.am.gov.br

Famous Picture:

Pará (PA)

Capital: Belém | Population: ~8,756,000 | Climate: Tropical

Description:

Pará features the Amazon River delta, mangroves, and mineral resources. Belém is known for its markets and cuisine. The state is a gateway to Amazon adventures and has significant mining and agriculture.

Official Link:

www.pa.gov.br

Famous Picture:

Rondônia (RO)

Capital: Porto Velho | Population: ~1,757,000 | Climate: Tropical

Description:

Rondônia is part of the Amazon arc, with rapid development from agriculture and cattle ranching. It has natural reserves and rivers for fishing. The economy is driven by soy, coffee, and timber.

Official Link:

www.ro.gov.br

Famous Picture:

Roraima (RR)

Capital: Boa Vista | Population: ~761,000 | Climate: Tropical savanna

Description:

The northernmost state, Roraima has savannas, mountains, and borders Venezuela and Guyana. It's known for Mount Roraima and indigenous communities. Agriculture and mining are key sectors.

Official Link:

www.rr.gov.br

Famous Picture:

Tocantins (TO)

Capital: Palmas | Population: ~1,596,000 | Climate: Tropical

Description:

The newest state, created in 1988, Tocantins has the Jalapão desert-like region and rivers. Palmas is modern. Economy includes soy farming, cattle, and tourism in natural areas.

Official Link:

www.to.gov.br

Capital: Maceió | Population: ~3,221,000 | Climate: Tropical

Description:

Alagoas is known for its beautiful beaches, lagoons, and coral reefs. The economy relies on tourism, sugar cane, and oil. Maceió is a coastal city with vibrant culture and seafood.

Description:

Bahia is the heart of Afro-Brazilian culture, with historic Salvador, beaches, and carnival. Economy includes tourism, oil, and agriculture. It's diverse with chapada plateaus and islands.

www.ba.gov.br

Ceará (CE)

Description:

Ceará boasts stunning beaches like Jericoacoara and wind sports. Fortaleza is a major city. Economy features tourism, textiles, and renewable energy from wind farms.

Official Link:

www.ce.gov.br

Description:

Maranhão has the Lençóis Maranhenses dunes, Amazon edges, and colonial São Luís. Economy includes aluminum, agriculture, and tourism. It's culturally rich with reggae and bumba-meu-boi.

www.ma.gov.br

Capital: João Pessoa | Population: ~4,183,000 | Climate: Tropical

Paraíba has beaches, dinosaur fossils, and the easternmost point of the Americas. João Pessoa is green and historic. Economy is based on tourism, agriculture, and services.
