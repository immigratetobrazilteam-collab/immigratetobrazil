Cost of Living in Brazil

Budget for your move and understand expenses by city

Overview

Brazil offers a wide range of living costs depending on your location, lifestyle, and choices. Major cities are more expensive, while smaller cities and rural areas offer significantly lower costs. This guide breaks down typical expenses to help you budget effectively.

Money-Saving Tips

1. Live Outside City Centers

Suburbs and neighborhoods outside downtown areas offer 30-50% cheaper rent while staying connected via public transport.

2. Use Public Transportation

Monthly metro/bus passes cost $30-$60. It's cheaper and efficient in major cities.

3. Shop at Markets & Fairs

Street markets (feiras) and larger supermarkets like Carrefour offer better prices than small shops.

4. Eat Like a Local

Seek out prato feito (plate of the day), street food, and local restaurants instead of tourist areas.

5. Use Healthcare Wisely

Brazil's public system (SUS) is free. Use it for non-urgent care and insure for emergencies.

6. Share Accommodation

Renting with roommates can cut housing costs by 40-60%.

7. Take Advantage of Free Activities

Beaches, parks, cultural events, and festivals are often free or very cheap.

Exchange Rates & Banking

Currency: Brazilian Real (BRL) - Exchange rate varies but typically 4.5-5.5 BRL per USD (check current rates)

Banking: Open accounts with Caixa Econômica Federal, Bradesco, or Itaú for better rates and services

Money Transfer: Use services like Wise, OFX, or PayPal for international transfers at better rates than banks

Tip: ATM withdrawals are convenient but check fees. Credit cards (cartão de crédito) are widely accepted and offer good rates
