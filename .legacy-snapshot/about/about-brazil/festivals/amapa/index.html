1. Festivals Overview

Amapá offers a distinctive cultural calendar shaped by Afro-Amazonian traditions, the Equator landmark, and lively riverine communities.

2. Festival Calendar (By Month)

  • January–February: Carnaval de Macapá, Festa de São Tiago
  • March–April: Semana Santa, Feira do Peixe
  • May: Festa do Trabalhador, Festival do Abacaxi
  • June: Arraial do Povo, Marabaixo Festival
  • July: ExpoAmapá, Festival do Camarão
  • August: Festa do Meio do Mundo, Festival de Música do Amapá
  • September: Semana da Pátria, Festival Literário
  • October–November: Semana da Consciência Negra, Festival de Dança
  • December: Natal da Família, Réveillon do Amapá

3. Top Annual Festivals (Flagship Events)

  • Marabaixo Festival: Afro-Amazonian rhythm, dance, and heritage.
  • Festa do Meio do Mundo: Celebrations at the Equator landmark.
  • ExpoAmapá: Trade, culture, and regional business showcase.
  • Carnaval de Macapá: Amazonian-style street carnival.
  • Festival do Camarão: Shrimp cuisine and local gastronomy.
  • Festa de São Tiago: Historic religious traditions.

5. Festival Types & Categories

Expect Afro-Amazonian, religious, food, music, and civic celebrations centered on Macapá.

6. Religious & Faith-Based Festivals

Festa de São Tiago and patron-saint festivities lead the faith-based calendar.

7. Music & Dance Festivals

Marabaixo and Festival de Música do Amapá spotlight percussion and dance traditions.

8. Folk & Traditional Celebrations

Arraial do Povo and regional June celebrations anchor traditional culture.

9. Food & Gastronomy Festivals

Festival do Camarão and Festival do Abacaxi are highlights for regional flavors.

10. Carnival & Pre-Carnival Events

Carnaval de Macapá brings blocos and parades to the capital.

11. Indigenous & Afro-Brazilian Festivals

Semana da Consciência Negra and Marabaixo preserve Afro-Amazonian heritage.

12. State & Municipal Holidays

Semana da Pátria and municipal holidays include civic parades and performances.

13. Festival History & Origins

Many festivals grew from river trade, colonial religious practices, and Afro-Brazilian resistance culture.

14. Cultural Significance & Symbolism

Amapá’s events symbolize Amazonian identity, the Equator’s presence, and Afro-Brazilian resilience.

15. Cost & Accessibility

Most celebrations are free; ticketed concerts appear during major fairs.

16. Where Festivals Take Place

Macapá is the main hub, with community events in riverside towns.

17. Best Time to Visit for Festivals

June–August offers the densest cultural programming.

18. Local Customs & Etiquette

Be respectful at religious events, and follow local guidance at Equator ceremonies.

19. Safety & Crowd Tips

Use official transport and stay hydrated in the tropical climate.

20. Family-Friendly vs Adult-Oriented Festivals

Most celebrations are family-friendly; concerts and late-night events skew adult.

21. Festival Food, Drinks & Traditions
