Start Your Immigration Journey

Step-by-step guidance to apply for your Brazilian visa

Application Steps

Step 1: Choose Your Visa Type

Your first decision determines everything else about your application. Review all visa options:

  • Work Visa: Have a job offer from a Brazilian employer
  • Investor Visa: Plan to invest and start a business
  • Family Visa: Have Brazilian family members
  • Rentista/Aposentado: Have passive income or retirement funds
  • Student Visa: Enrolled in a Brazilian institution
  • Remote Work: Work for international company remotely

Action: Review full visa guide

Step 2: Gather & Prepare Documents

Universal Documents (ALL visas):

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity)
  • Birth certificate (certified copy)
  • Criminal background check from your country
  • Medical examination (usually at designated clinic)
  • Recent passport-sized photographs

Visa-Specific Documents:

  • Work: Job offer letter, employer sponsorship document
  • Investor: Business plan, investment proof, bank statements
  • Family: Marriage/birth certificates, sponsorship letter
  • Financial: Income statements, bank account records

Important:

Order certified copies of all documents. Apostille documents (Hague Apostille) in your country of origin.

Step 3: Translate Documents to Portuguese

Critical: All documents must be officially translated to Portuguese

How to Translate:

  1. Find a certified translator (juramentado) in your city
  2. Submit documents for translation
  3. Receive official translated documents with translator's stamp
  4. Get apostille certification if required

Costs:

  • Professional translation: $10-30 per document
  • Apostille service: $5-20 per document (varies by country)
  • Total estimate: $200-500

Timeline:

Allow 2-4 weeks for full translation and certification

Step 4: Locate Your Brazilian Consulate

Find the consulate jurisdiction for your home address

How to Find:

  1. Visit Ministry of Foreign Affairs: www.gov.br/mre
  2. Search for "Brazilian consulate [your city]"
  3. Check official list of consulates
  4. Note address, phone number, and website

Consulate Locations (major cities):

  • North America: New York, Los Angeles, Miami, Toronto, Mexico City
  • Europe: London, Paris, Berlin, Madrid, Rome, Lisbon
  • Asia: Tokyo, Hong Kong, Shanghai, Delhi
  • Others: Australia, Middle East, Africa, South America

Note:

You must apply at the consulate covering your residence, not your citizenship

Step 5: Schedule Appointment & Submit Application

Scheduling:

  1. Contact consulate (phone, email, or online system)
  2. Request appointment for visa application
  3. Confirm required documents and fees
  4. Schedule at least 2-4 weeks in advance

At Your Appointment:

  • Bring all original documents and translated copies
  • Bring completed visa application form
  • Bring passport and identification
  • Be prepared to answer questions about your application
  • Pay visa fee (usually $50-200)

What to Expect:

Interview typically lasts 10-30 minutes. Consulate staff assess your application for completeness and eligibility.

Step 6: Wait for Visa Approval

Processing Timeline:

  • Work Visa: 2-4 weeks
  • Investor Visa: 4-8 weeks
  • Family Visa: 4-12 weeks
  • Student Visa: 2-4 weeks
  • Rentista Visa: 3-6 weeks

During Wait:

  • Consulate may request additional documents
  • Respond promptly if contacted
  • Follow up if you don't hear after stated timeline
  • Don't book flights until visa is approved

Step 7: Receive Visa & Travel to Brazil

When Approved:

  • Consulate notifies you (by phone, email)
  • Pick up your passport with visa stamp
  • Check visa validity dates carefully
  • Verify all information is correct

Traveling:

  • Book flight for within visa validity period
  • Bring: Passport with visa, return ticket, hotel reservations
  • Have contact information for your employer/sponsor
  • Bring translations of important documents

Important:

Most visas expire 90 days after issue. You must travel within this window.

Step 8: Arrive & Register in Brazil

Upon Arrival:

  • Pass through immigration at airport
  • Officer stamps your passport
  • You now have temporary residence in Brazil

Within 30 Days:

  1. Register with Federal Police (Polícia Federal)
  2. Provide proof of address (rental contract, hotel receipt)
  3. Receive RNE (Carteira de Residente - residency card)
  4. Register for CPF (tax ID)

First Week Tasks:

  • Find accommodation
  • Open bank account
  • Get SIM card/phone number
  • Familiarize yourself with neighborhood

Application Timeline Summary

Month 1-2: Preparation

Gather and prepare all documents, get translations and apostille

Month 2-3: Application

Schedule consulate appointment and submit application

Month 3-6: Processing

Wait for visa approval (varies by visa type)

Month 6: Travel

Receive visa, book flights, prepare for move

Month 7: Arrival

Travel to Brazil and begin your new life

Pre-Application Checklist

  • ✓ Chosen visa type
  • ✓ Gathered all documents
  • ✓ Translated documents to Portuguese
  • ✓ Got apostille certification
  • ✓ Located correct consulate
  • ✓ Contacted consulate for requirements
  • ✓ Scheduled appointment

Documents to Bring to Consulate

  • Original passport
  • Original birth certificate
  • Translated documents
  • Visa application form (completed)
  • Photographs
  • Criminal background check
  • Medical examination results
  • Proof of financial resources
  • Visa fee (check amount)

Questions to Ask Consulate

  • What are exact document requirements?
  • Do I need an appointment?
  • What is the visa fee?
  • What is current processing time?
  • What format for documents?
  • Do I need authorization from Brazil first?
  • Can I do anything online?
