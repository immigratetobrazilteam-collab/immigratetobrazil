Overview

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and the choices you have. It applies to this website and any pages that link to this policy.

Note: This policy is provided for general information and is not legal advice.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function, remember your preferences, and understand how the site is used.

Cookies can be session-based (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent (stored for a set period).

Types of cookies we use

Strictly necessary

Required for core site functions like security, navigation, and form submission. These cannot be disabled.

Performance & analytics

Help us understand how visitors use the site (pages visited, time on page, errors) so we can improve performance.

Preferences

Remember choices like language, region, or layout to provide a more personalized experience.

Marketing

Used to measure campaign performance and show relevant content. These are only used if enabled where required.

Why we use cookies

  • To keep the site secure and available.
  • To remember your preferences and improve the user experience.
  • To understand how the site is used and improve content and performance.
  • To measure outreach and marketing effectiveness where applicable.

First-party vs. third-party cookies

First-party cookies are set by this website to provide core functionality and remember your settings.

Third-party cookies are set by services we use (for example, analytics) and may collect usage data across sites.

Cookies and similar technologies

We may use cookies, local storage, and similar technologies to deliver site functionality and improve services. Some third-party tools may set their own cookies.

Third-party services

We may use third-party analytics or performance tools. These providers may set cookies in your browser. Their use of cookies is governed by their own policies.

Cookie categories in detail

Necessary cookies

Support login sessions, security protections, and essential navigation features. Disabling these can break site features.

Analytics cookies

Measure site performance, error rates, and page engagement to help us improve the website.

Preferences cookies

Store language, region, or accessibility choices so you don’t have to re-select them on each visit.

Marketing cookies

Help measure campaign performance and show more relevant content where these features are enabled.

Examples of data collected

  • Pages viewed and navigation paths.
  • Approximate location derived from IP address (country or region).
  • Device type, browser version, and screen resolution.
  • Session duration and error events.

We do not use cookies to collect sensitive personal information without your involvement.

How long cookies last

Session cookies expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain until they expire or are deleted.

Where possible, we limit cookie duration to what is necessary for the intended purpose.

How we set cookies

Cookies may be set when you:

  • Visit a page that embeds third-party services.
  • Submit forms or interact with site features.
  • Accept or adjust cookie preferences where available.

Consent and controls

Where required, you will be given options to accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can also withdraw consent by changing your browser settings or clearing cookies.

Necessary cookies are always active because they are required for the site to function.

Legal bases (general)

We use cookies based on legitimate interests (such as security and site performance) and, where applicable, consent for non-essential categories like marketing or analytics.

This section is informational and does not constitute legal advice.

LGPD context (informational)

Brazil’s LGPD provides rights related to personal data. If cookies or similar technologies are used to process personal data, the site should provide transparency, lawful basis, and ways to exercise rights.

For compliance guidance, consult qualified professionals.

Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a “Do Not Track” (DNT) setting. When enabled, we aim to limit non-essential tracking where feasible.

Managing your preferences

  • Browser controls: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies in settings.
  • Device controls: Mobile devices have privacy settings that limit tracking.
  • Opt-out tools: Some analytics providers offer opt-out mechanisms on their websites.

If you disable cookies, some features may not work as intended.

Browser help links

  • Chrome: Manage cookies in Settings > Privacy and security.
  • Firefox: Manage cookies in Settings > Privacy & Security.
  • Safari: Manage cookies in Settings > Privacy.
  • Edge: Manage cookies in Settings > Cookies and site permissions.
