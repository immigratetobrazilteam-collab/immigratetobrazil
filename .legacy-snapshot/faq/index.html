Getting started

Where do I begin if I want to move to Brazil? +

Start by defining your purpose (work, study, family, retirement, investment) and timeline. Then identify the correct visa pathway, gather required documents, and confirm local registration steps after arrival.

Do I need a visa to enter Brazil? +

Requirements depend on your nationality and purpose of stay. Short visits may be visa‑free for some nationalities, while long‑term stays typically require a visa or residence authorization.

How long does the process usually take? +

Timelines vary by visa type and documentation readiness. Plan for several weeks to a few months, plus time to gather documents, translations, and apostilles where required.

Visas and residency

What are the most common visa categories? +

Common pathways include work visas, student visas, family reunification, retirement, digital nomad, and investment-based residence. Each has specific eligibility criteria.

Can I work on a tourist visa? +

No. Working on a tourist visa is not permitted. You must hold a visa or residence authorization that allows work.

Do I need a job offer before applying for a work visa? +

Most work visas require an employer sponsor and a job offer. Some specialized or independent activity categories may have different requirements.

How do I transition from temporary to permanent residency? +

Paths vary, but may include time in status, family ties, or other criteria. Keep records of lawful residence and follow local renewal timelines.

Documents and legalization

What documents are commonly required? +

Typically: valid passport, application forms, proof of income or support, police clearance, proof of address, and purpose‑specific documents (job offer, enrollment, marriage certificate).

Do I need translations and apostilles? +

Many documents must be apostilled in the issuing country and translated into Portuguese by a certified translator in Brazil. Verify exact requirements for your case.

How long are documents valid? +

Some documents (like police clearances) have short validity windows. Plan document timing to avoid expiration.

Arrival and registration

What is a CPF and why do I need it? +

The CPF is Brazil’s tax ID. It’s required for banking, rentals, utilities, mobile plans, and many services.

Do I need to register with the Federal Police? +

Yes, most long‑term visas require registration after arrival. Deadlines apply, so book appointments promptly.

Cost of living and budgeting

How much does it cost to live in Brazil? +

Costs vary widely by city and lifestyle. Major metros are more expensive than smaller cities. Build a realistic monthly budget for rent, utilities, food, transport, and healthcare.

Do I need proof of income? +

Many visa categories and rentals require proof of income or financial means. Requirements depend on the visa type and local policies.

Housing

Is it hard to rent an apartment? +

Some landlords request a guarantor or proof of income. Short‑term rentals are easier while you establish local documents.

Should I buy or rent? +

Most newcomers rent first. Buying can be considered after you understand neighborhoods, costs, and legal requirements.

Healthcare

Can foreigners use public healthcare (SUS)? +

Public healthcare is available, but coverage and wait times vary. Many residents supplement with private health plans.

Is private healthcare expensive? +

Costs depend on age, coverage, and city. Compare plans carefully and confirm network coverage near your location.

Work and taxes

Can I work remotely for a foreign employer? +

Remote work may be permitted under specific visa categories. Ensure you meet the requirements and understand tax obligations.

Do I need to pay taxes in Brazil? +

Tax residency depends on length of stay and other factors. Consult a tax professional to understand your obligations.

Education and family

Can my children attend school in Brazil? +

Yes. Public and private schools are available, but enrollment requirements vary by location and school type.

Can family members join me? +

Many visas allow dependents, but requirements differ. Confirm eligibility and document needs for each family member.

Safety, language, and culture

Do I need to speak Portuguese? +

Portuguese helps with daily life and official processes. In major cities, you may find English support, but basic Portuguese is strongly recommended.

Is Brazil safe? +

Safety varies by city and neighborhood. Use local guidance, avoid risky areas, and follow common‑sense precautions.

Practical setup

How do I open a bank account? +

Most banks require a CPF and proof of address. Digital banks may have simpler onboarding for newcomers.

How do I get a SIM card? +

Prepaid SIMs are available at airports, malls, and carrier stores. A CPF is often required for activation.

Legal and compliance

Are immigration rules the same in every state? +

Federal rules are consistent, but local procedures and appointment availability vary. Always confirm with local offices.

Should I use an attorney or consultant? +

Complex cases benefit from professional guidance. Ensure any provider is reputable and transparent about fees.
