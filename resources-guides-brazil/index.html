Resources & Guides

Tools and information to support your immigration journey

Essential Government Resources

Federal Police (Polícia Federal)

Brazilian government immigration authority. Handles visas, residency permits, and immigration matters.

Website: www.gov.br/pf (Portuguese)

Services: Visa information, residency registration, passport services

Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Itamaraty)

Diplomatic service managing consulates and visa processing worldwide.

Website: www.gov.br/mre

Find: Brazilian consulate locations, visa application procedures

Brazilian Consulates

Apply for visas at your nearest Brazilian consulate. Locations worldwide.

How to Find: Search "Brazilian consulate [your city]"

Services: Visa applications, authentication, consular services

Banco do Brasil / Caixa Bank

Major banks for opening accounts and financial services.

Requirements: Valid visa, passport, address proof

Services: Checking, savings, investments, loans

Planning & Preparation Guides

Document Checklist

Essential Documents:

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity)
  • Birth certificate (certified copy, Portuguese translation)
  • Marriage/divorce certificate (if applicable)
  • Criminal background check
  • Medical examination/health certificate
  • Proof of financial resources
  • Employment letter (if applicable)
  • Recent photographs (passport style)

Visa-Specific Documents:

  • Job offer letter (work visa)
  • Investment proof (investor visa)
  • Marriage certificate (family visa)
  • University admission (student visa)
  • Retirement/income proof (rentista visa)

Document Translation & Authentication

Important: All documents must be officially translated to Portuguese by certified translators.

Steps:

  1. Use a certified translator (juramentado) in your country
  2. Get documents apostilled (Hague Apostille)
  3. Keep originals for reference
  4. Carry translated copies to Brazil

Cost:

Translation typically $10-30 per document; apostille service varies by country

Timeline & Application Process

Typical Timeline:

  1. Month 1-2: Prepare documents and translations
  2. Month 2-3: Schedule consulate appointment
  3. Month 3-4: Submit application at consulate
  4. Month 4-6: Wait for visa approval
  5. Month 6: Receive visa stamp
  6. Month 6-7: Travel to Brazil
  7. Within 30 days: Register with Federal Police

Variations:

Timeline varies by visa type. Work/investor visas often faster than family visas

Practical Guides

Opening a Bank Account

  • Required documents: Passport, visa, address proof
  • CPF registration first (tax ID)
  • Popular banks: Caixa, Bradesco, Itaú, BB
  • Online platforms: Nubank, Inter, Wise
  • Minimum deposits vary (usually $50-500)

Getting Your CPF

CPF (Cadastro de Pessoas Físicas) is essential for all activities in Brazil

  • Apply at Federal Revenue Service (Receita Federal)
  • Bring: Passport, address proof, completed form
  • Takes 1-2 weeks usually
  • Essential for: Work, banking, contracts, taxes

Finding Accommodation

  • Online platforms: Airbnb, Booking, Imóvel Web
  • Neighborhoods: Research before moving
  • Costs: Include utilities in budget
  • Contracts: Typically 12 months
  • Deposits: Usually 1-3 months rent

Healthcare in Brazil

  • Public (SUS): Free but often slow
  • Private insurance: $50-150/month recommended
  • Pharmacies: Widespread, many meds over-the-counter
  • Hospitals: Good quality in major cities

Language & Communication

  • Portuguese essential (most don't speak English)
  • Language apps: Duolingo, Babbel, iTalki
  • Classes: Available in most cities
  • Immersion: Best way to learn quickly

Tax Obligations

  • Register with Federal Revenue Service
  • Foreign income may be taxable
  • Annual tax return (IRPF) required
  • Consult accountant for details
